This is a fantastic plan. Southern Poland is perfect for teenagers (14 and 18) because it balances "kiddie" fun with serious adrenaline.

Since you are looking for an **hour south of Kraków** in a cabin, you are likely looking at the area around **Myślenice, Rabka-Zdrój, or Mszana Dolna**, or perhaps pushing closer to **Zakopane** (which is usually 1.5 - 2 hours from Kraków due to traffic).

Here is a curated list of things to do tailored for teenagers who like theme parks and water activities.

### 1. The Ultimate Theme Park: Energylandia

You mentioned your kids like "stuff like Legoland." For ages 14 and 18, Legoland might actually be too young/boring. What they really want is **Energylandia**.

* **What it is:** The #1 Amusement Park in Europe for rollercoasters. It is massive.
* **Why for teens:** It has an "Extreme Zone" with world-class record-breaking coasters (like the *Hyperion* and *Zadra*). It is genuine thrill-seeking territory that will impress an 18-year-old.
* **Location:** Zator (about 45 mins - 1 hour west of Kraków). It is easily drivable from the mountains.
* **Tip:** It includes a water park zone inside, so bring swimsuits.

### 2. The Best "Mountain Waterparks" (Thermal Baths)

In the Polish mountains, "waterparks" are actually huge geothermal complexes called *Termy*. They are modern, clean, and have both relaxation zones and slide zones.

* **Chochołowskie Termy (Top Pick):** The largest complex. It has a massive zone with slides, outdoor warm pools with mountain views, and swim-up bars (for the 18-year-old, the legal drinking age is 18).
* **Terma Bania (in Białka Tatrzańska):** Also fantastic, located right next to a ski slope/green hill. Very lively atmosphere.
* **Gorący Potok:** This is an outdoor thermal bath style that looks like a "Japanese Onsen" made of natural wood. It’s cooler/trendier and often has DJs in the evenings.

### 3. Mountain Adventures (Adrenaline & Views)

Since you are staying in a cabin, you are likely near the Tatra or Beskid mountains.

* **Dunajec River Rafting (Pontoons):**
* *Traditional:* Wooden rafts steered by highlanders (relaxed, scenic).
* *Teen option:* Rent inflatable kayaks or pontoons for a slightly more active trip down the river gorge. The scenery is spectacular (limestone cliffs).


* **Gubałówka & Alpine Slides (Zakopane):**
* Take the funicular train up Gubałówka mountain.
* At the top, there is a **Gravity Slide** (Alpine Coaster) where they can race down in carts.
* There is also a "highlander market" at the bottom which is fun for food/souvenirs.


* **Quads / ATVs:**
* Because it is a mountain region, there are many operators offering Quad Bike tours through the mud and forests. This is usually a hit with teenagers.



### 4. If you go into Kraków (City Day Trip)

If you drive north into the city for a day, skip the boring museums. Kraków has specific spots that teenagers love:

* **Lost Souls Alley:** An interactive horror museum/haunted house. It is famous and genuinely scary. You walk through dark mazes with actors chasing you. (Check reviews first to see if your 14-year-old can handle it; the 18-year-old will likely love/hate it).
* **Kraków Pinball Museum:** A cool underground cellar filled with unlimited-play vintage arcade and pinball machines.
* **Kazimierz District (Plac Nowy):** Go here for the best street food in Poland—*Zapiekanka* (huge open-faced baguette pizzas).

### Summary Itinerary Idea

* **Day 1:** **Energylandia** (Full day, leave early).
* **Day 2:** **Mountain Chill.** Go to Zakopane, take the Gubałówka train, ride the gravity slide, walk Krupówki street.
* **Day 3:** **Water Relaxation.** Spend the afternoon/evening at **Chochołowskie Termy** to recover.
* **Day 4:** **Adventure.** Pontoons on the Dunajec River or a Quad bike tour near your cabin.

### A Note on Driving

The road from Kraków to Zakopane (called the "Zakopianka") is famous for traffic jams, especially on weekends and holidays.

* **Cabin Location Tip:** If you want to be "an hour south of Kraków," look for cabins in the **Rabka-Zdrój** or **Mszana Dolna** area. This puts you halfway between the city and the high mountains (Zakopane), making it easier to drive to Energylandia (west) or the Thermal Baths (south) without getting stuck in the worst deep-mountain traffic.

Here is an analysis of your two cabin locations and a list of adjusted activities.

### 1. Location Battle: Paszkówka vs. Przysłop

**Option A: Paszkówka (The "Convenient" Choice)**

* **Vibe:** This is **not** the high mountains. It is in the foothills (rolling green farmland). It will feel rural and peaceful, but you won't have dramatic mountain peaks towering over you.
* **Pros:** It is strategically perfect for your needs. It is very close to **Energylandia** and **Inwałd Park** (see below). It is also closer to Kraków, making the drive from the airport easier.
* **Cons:** Less "alpine" atmosphere.

**Option B: Przysłop (The "Real Mountain" Choice)**

* *Note: There are a few places named Przysłop, but usually cabin rentals are near **Zawoja**.*
* **Vibe:** This is typically deep in the mountains (Beskidy range). It is quieter, surrounded by forests, and feels like a true "cabin in the woods."
* **Pros:** Very calm, secluded, beautiful nature, low sensory input (quiet nights).
* **Cons:** The roads can be narrow/steep (fun for some, stressful for others). It is a longer drive to the theme parks (approx. 45-60 mins to Inwałd/Energylandia).

**Verdict:**

* Choose **Paszkówka** if you want short drives to the attractions.
* Choose **Przysłop** if the priority is sitting by a fire in a silent forest and you don't mind driving further for day trips.

### 2. "Calmer" & Younger-Skewing Attractions

Since they like "stuff like Legoland" and younger themes, Southern Poland has excellent "Miniature Parks" and open-air exhibits which are often hits for teens with ASD because they are visual, detailed, and allow for walking at your own pace without the pressure of a ride queue.

#### **Top Pick: Inwałd Park (near Paszkówka)**

This is the best alternative to Energylandia. It is actually 5 parks in one location.

* **Miniature Park:** Famous buildings from around the world built at 1:25 scale. It is very orderly and visual—great for walking through and looking at details (Eiffel Tower, Great Wall of China, etc.).
* **Dinolandia:** Life-size dinosaur statues. It’s geared younger, but many teens with ASD love the specific details of the dinosaurs.
* **Why it fits:** It is much calmer than Energylandia. It has wide paths and is focused on looking/exploring rather than screaming on coasters.

#### **Chabówka Railway Museum (Skansen Taboru Kolejowego)**

Located near Rabka-Zdrój.

* **What it is:** An open-air museum full of old steam locomotives, trains, and snowplows.
* **Why it fits:** You can climb inside the old trains. It is tactile, mechanical, and usually very quiet on weekdays. If your kids like trains or machinery, this is a paradise.

#### **Rabkoland (in Rabka-Zdrój)**

* **What it is:** A theme park strictly for children (ages 2-12 usually), BUT it has a very low "thrill" level.
* **Why it fits:** If your 14 and 18-year-olds genuinely prefer "younger" rides (gentle carousels, slow boats, moving statues), they will feel safe here. It is very colorful and whimsical. However, check pictures first to ensure they won't feel it is *too* babyish.

#### **Open Air Museum in Zubrzyca Górna (Orawski Park Etnograficzny)**

* **What it is:** A preserved 17th-century village. Wooden cottages, old mills, farm equipment.
* **Why it fits:** It is a huge open space with very few people. It’s incredibly calming. You walk through old houses, smell the wood and dry grass. It’s "educational" but in a sensory-friendly, atmospheric way.

### 3. Revised Waterpark Strategy

The big "Termy" (thermal baths) can be loud echo chambers with hundreds of people.

* **Better Option:** **Termy Szaflary**. It is smaller and older than the giant "Chochołowskie" or "Bania," meaning it is often less crowded and quieter.
* **Timing:** Go **as soon as they open** (usually 9:00 AM) or **late evening** (after 7:00 PM). The middle of the day is the loudest.

### 4. A "Safe" Mountain Experience

If hiking up a steep peak is too stressful or tiring:

* **Dolina Kościeliska (Kościeliska Valley):** This is in the Tatra National Park. It is a flat, wide gravel path that runs along a stream at the bottom of the mountains. You are *in* the mountains, looking up at cliffs, but you are just walking on a flat road. It is very predictable and safe. Horse carriages also run here if they get tired.

### Summary Recommendation

I suggest booking **Paszkówka**.
It puts you 15 minutes from **Inwałd Park** (Miniatures/Dinos) and 20 minutes from **Energylandia** (if they decide they want the big rides). It gives you a "safe base" that is easy to access, reducing travel stress.
